# `<stanza>`

The `<stanza>` tag groups lines inside of a [`<poem>`](poem).

## Syntax

<SyntaxDisplay name="stanza" />

## Examples

```ptx-example /stanza/
<FRAGMENT parents="">
   <poem>
       <title>Fire and Ice</title>
       <idx>Fire and Ice, Frost</idx>
       <author>Robert Frost</author>
       <stanza>
           <line>Some say the world will end in fire,</line>
           <line>Some say in ice.</line>
           <line>From what I've tasted of desire</line>
           <line>I hold with those who favor fire.</line>
           <line>But if it had to perish twice,</line>
           <line>I think I know enough of hate</line>
           <line>To say that for destruction ice</line>
           <line>Is also great</line>
           <line>And would suffice.</line>
       </stanza>
   </poem>
</FRAGMENT>
```

A `<stanza>` can have a `<title>` element, which is displayed above the lines of the stanza.

```ptx-example /stanza/
<FRAGMENT parents="">
   <poem>
       <title>My Poem</title>
       <stanza>
           <title>Beginnings</title>
           <line>It starts with number one;</line>
           <line>then on to number two.</line>
           <line>But what I take away</line>
           <line>is the start of something new.</line>
       </stanza>
       <stanza>
           <title>Endings</title>
           <line>After time has come and passed,</line>
           <line>I fear not much will stand to last.</line>
           <line>Farewell and goodbye is too much to say.</line>
           <line>Instead I just walk away.</line>
       </stanza>
   </poem>
</FRAGMENT>
```
