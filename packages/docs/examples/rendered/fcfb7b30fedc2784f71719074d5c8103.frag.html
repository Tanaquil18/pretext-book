Subsection 1.1

Example 1.1.1. Linear Maps.

Let \(P_n(\mathbb R)\) denote the real vector space of polynomials of degree at most \(n\text{.}\) The map \(T:P_n(\mathbb R) \to P_n(\mathbb R)\) given by \(T(p) = p'\text{,}\) taking a polynomial to its derivative, is a linear map.

Hint 1.

What do you know about how the derivative affects the degree of a polynomial and what did you learn in calculus about the derivative of a sum of differentiable functions?

Hint 2.

How about the derivative of a constant times a differentiable function?
