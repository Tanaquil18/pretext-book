Subsection 1.1

Problem 1.1.1. Row and column spaces.

We know that the rank of a matrix \(A\) and of its row-reduced echelon form \(R\) is the same. Determine if the row space and column space of \(A\) and \(R\) are always the same.

Hint.

Elementary row operations are invertible transformations of the rows of a matrix, not the columns.

Answer.

The row space of \(A\) and \(R\) are always the same. The column spaces are generally not equal.

Solution.

As in the hint, each elementary row operation is invertible showing the row spaces are identical. If \(A=R\text{,}\) then the column spaces are obviously equal, but if
\begin{equation*} A = \begin{bmatrix}1\amp1\\1\amp 1\end{bmatrix}, \text{ then } R = \begin{bmatrix}1\amp1\\0\amp 0\end{bmatrix}, \end{equation*}
so the column spaces are different lines in \(\mathbb R^2.\)
